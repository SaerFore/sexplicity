Official genre definition

What is swaycore?

Swaycore is a groove-first alternative rock genre derived directly from swancore.

It preserves swancore’s technical and melodic guitar language while shifting the center of the arrangement toward bass movement, pocket-driven drums, physical repetition and expressive vocal phrasing.

Swaycore is developed and defined by Sexplicity.

Definition

Groove is not an addition.
It is the structure.

Swaycore reorganizes the technical language of swancore around physical movement. Guitars remain melodic, rhythmic and technically detailed, but they operate alongside an active rhythm section rather than remaining continuously dominant.

Swaycore is a groove-first alternative rock genre that combines technical melodic guitars, active bass lines, pocket-focused drums, controlled rhythmic repetition and expressive vocals. Its music can move between intimacy, sensuality, urgency and heaviness without abandoning its rhythmic identity.

Musical principles

Core characteristics

  • Groove is structural. Bass, drums, guitars and vocal phrasing are organized around movement, pocket and rhythmic tension.
  • Guitars retain the swancore lineage. Clean figures, melodic riffs, bright arpeggios, warm distortion and technical rhythmic accents remain central.
  • The rhythm section has an active role. Bass movement and pocket-driven drumming create the physical center of the music rather than functioning as simple support.
  • Complexity is selective. Technical detail serves the groove, emotional direction and memorability of the song instead of filling every available space.
  • Vocals remain unrestricted. Swaycore is not defined by the vocalist’s gender, identity or one required vocal technique. Vocals may be clean, intense, textured, restrained or aggressive as long as they interact meaningfully with the groove.
  • The emotional range remains open. The genre may be sensual, intimate, dark, playful, energetic, urgent or heavy while retaining its emphasis on physical rhythm.

Musical lineage

Swaycore and swancore

Swaycore does not remove the musical foundation of swancore. It changes the hierarchy inside the arrangement.

What it preserves

Technical melodic guitars, fluid riffs, bright arpeggios, rhythmic cuts, dynamic contrast and the progressive post-hardcore foundation associated with swancore.

What it reorganizes

The groove, bass movement, drum pocket, repetition and vocal phrasing receive greater structural weight, while guitar complexity becomes more selective and rhythmically purposeful.

First published expressions

Swaycore in the Sexplicity catalog

The foundations of swaycore were developed across Sexplicity’s first EP, Body Language, and its first full-length concept album, No Love Fest.

These releases established the relationship between technical guitars, groove-led arrangements, intimacy, rhythmic tension and selective complexity that defines the genre.
