Sexplicity

Sexplicity is a production-led music project developed through the creative identities of Eli Ruffo and Saer Fore.

Rooted in swancore, the project develops and defines swaycore: a groove-first alternative rock genre built around technical melodic guitars, rhythmic movement, expressive vocals and controlled intensity.

Sexplicity places sound, production and concept before the idea of a conventional band.

The Sexplicity sound

Groove first.
Tension always.

Sexplicity approaches swancore through movement rather than constant instrumental density. The guitars remain technical, melodic and unmistakably rooted in the genre, but the songs are arranged around deep bass, pocket-driven drums, recurring rhythmic figures and expressive vocal phrasing.

Desire, intimacy and emotional distance are not treated only as lyrical subjects. They shape the pauses, repetitions, shifts in intensity and physical movement of the music itself.

Genre definition

What is swaycore?

Swaycore is a groove-first alternative rock genre derived directly from swancore. It preserves swancore’s technical and melodic guitar language while reorganizing the arrangement around pocket, bass movement, physical repetition and expressive vocal phrasing.

  • Groove is structural. The rhythm does not merely support the song; it determines how the instruments and vocals move.
  • Guitars remain technical and recognizable. Clean figures, melodic riffs, warm distortion, rhythmic accents and bright arpeggios carry the swancore lineage.
  • Bass and drums create the physical center. Active bass lines, strong pocket and controlled cuts give the music weight, movement and tension.
  • Complexity is selective. Swaycore values memorable detail and rhythmic purpose over nonstop instrumental density.
  • The emotional range stays open. The sound can be intimate, sensual, dark, playful, urgent, danceable or heavy without losing its rhythmic identity.

EP · January 31, 2026

Body Language

The first Sexplicity EP explores physical communication, attraction and hesitation: the moments in which words stop being enough and the body begins to answer in their place.

Across two connected tracks, intimacy is expressed through proximity, tension, rhythm and the reluctance to end the moment.

Album · February 14, 2026

No Love Fest

No Love Fest is an eight-track concept album that follows a single intimate connection from beginning to end. What starts with rules and an illusion of control gradually becomes repetition, dependence, self-deception and emotional consequence.

The album traces that progression through attraction, compulsive return, nightly obsession, physical urgency, confrontation, incomplete separation and the echo that remains afterward.
